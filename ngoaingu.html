Part 3 — Grammar: Used to / Be used to / Get used to

Used to + V: hành động trong quá khứ nhưng bây giờ không làm nữa.
Example: I used to eat a lot of fast food, but now I eat healthier.

Be used to + V-ing / N: quen với việc gì (state).

Get used to + V-ing / N: trở nên quen với việc gì (process).

Examples:
- She is used to getting up early.
- After moving to the city, he got used to noisy traffic.
Practice
  1. Complete: "I ___ (use) to smoke, but I don't now."
  2. Rewrite: "It was hard at first; now I'm comfortable with it." → "I ___ to it."

Part 5 — Reading
Reading text — A long and healthy life

Modern research shows that combining a balanced diet, regular exercise and enough sleep can significantly increase life expectancy. Communities that support healthy routines and reduce stress tend to enjoy higher well-being. Prevention — through vaccines and early diagnosis — is a key factor in avoiding chronic diseases.

Comprehension questions
  1. What three factors are mentioned as increasing life expectancy?
  2. Why are communities important for well-being?
  3. What role does prevention play?
Part 6 — Writing
Writing task

Write a short paragraph (120–150 words): "How to build healthy habits at school". Use vocabulary from the Unit and include at least one grammar structure from the Grammar section (Used to / Be used to / Get used to).

Model answer (example)

To build healthy habits at school, students should create a balanced routine that includes regular exercise and quality sleep. I used to stay up late, but now I am used to going to bed earlier. Eating nutritious meals and staying hydrated help improve concentration and overall well-being. Schools can support students by promoting physical activities and providing healthy food options.
